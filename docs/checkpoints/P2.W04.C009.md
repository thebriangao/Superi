# P2.W04.C009

## Outcome

Implemented graph-native peak, RMS, true-peak, phase, spectrum, and loudness metering. The meter
preserves exact samples, timing, semantic channel order, routing, and continuity while publishing
coherent control-side snapshots.

## Architecture and integration

`superi-audio::metering` owns a transparent single-input `AudioProcessor` prepared outside the
callback with bounded DSP, history, spectrum, and atomic publication storage. Its audio-domain path
copies input to output unchanged, performs per-channel and programme analysis, and publishes a
seqlock-protected snapshot without allocation or locks. The independent reader constructs owned
channel and spectrum results and evaluates the bounded integrated loudness gate on the control side.

## Implementation process

The public graph-consumer contract was written and observed failing before the metering API existed.
Primary ITU and EBU specifications established K weighting, four-phase true-peak interpolation,
channel weighting, 400 ms momentary and 3 s short-term windows, and absolute plus relative programme
gating. Implementation then added strict preparation bounds, fallible allocation, explicit history
saturation, coherent lock-free publication, and classified validation failures.

## Verification

Focused tests place the meter between a real source and master bus and prove transparent output,
exact sample coordinates, semantic channel order, peak, RMS, inter-sample true peak, phase, spectrum,
calibrated loudness windows, integrated gating, and preparation bounds. The final audit also runs the
complete audio package, formatting, targeted warnings-denied lint, map validation, and the checkpoint
verifier within the required 180-second speed-first timebox.

## Delivery

This record ships with the prepared meter, public consumer contract, and refreshed codebase maps.
The delivered commit is pushed to `origin/main`, and its full SHA is recorded in the paired checklist
after remote containment is confirmed.

## Remaining constraints

The engine does not yet bind decoded media and timeline slices through graph metering to device or
export delivery. Plugin hosting, physical hardware calibration, long-soak proof, and production mix
integration remain owned by later checkpoints and platform lanes.
