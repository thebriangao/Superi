# P2.W06.C004

## Outcome

Implemented one playback-domain transport owner for exact seeking, superseding scrubbing, bounded
looping, reduced signed speeds, direction changes, frame stepping, and explicit late-frame policy.
Interactive discontinuities cancel stale foreground and prediction work immediately, retain exact
frame coordinates, reanchor clock-to-frame cadence without jumps, and prevent pre-discontinuity
audio from leaking into the new transport epoch.

Playback, graph evaluation, cache population, display conversion, clock pacing, audio admission,
and bounded viewport delivery remain the same coherent engine established by P2.W06.C003. The new
policy changes only which ordinary interactive frame is scheduled and when it becomes due. It does
not change graph results, cache identity, color or alpha meaning, authored state, or the exact frame
path the separate render and export owner consumes. Live deadline and dropped-frame decisions never
enter that owner's immutable media timing or output artifacts.

## Architecture and integration

`PlaybackTransport<O>` owns paused, playing, scrubbing, and ended state over the existing
`PlaybackOrchestrator<O>` and `PlaybackPrefetcher`. Its immutable snapshot exposes the exact
playhead, owned frame, distinct shared-clock deadline, reduced signed `PlaybackRate`, loop range,
discontinuity epoch, cumulative drop evidence, audio state, and stable degraded-condition codes.
Every operation requires `ExecutionDomain::Playback`, and deterministic job identities combine one
caller-owned namespace with a checked local counter.

Transport frame coordinates remain integral in the caller's frame timebase. A separate
`(clock_anchor, frame_anchor)` mapping converts cadence with checked `i128` rational arithmetic.
Frame-to-clock conversion rounds upward so a frame is never presented early; clock-to-frame
selection rounds down so polling frequency cannot accumulate drift. At 0.5x, adjacent 24 fps frames
are due 4,000 samples apart on a 48 kHz clock. At 1.5x, they are due 1,334 samples apart. Rates above
one retain every frame as eligible unless the explicit drop policy skips an ordinary late frame.

`PlaybackOrchestrator::submit_frame_at_with_duration` separates exact scene identity and nominal
media duration from the transport's live presentation deadline and rate-adjusted interval. The
existing A/V coordinator schedules against those live values while every outcome retains the
immutable frame PTS and nominal duration. Each foreground request owns a cancellation token checked
before and after evaluation, and cancelling transport releases both in-flight and retained output
immediately. A result that finishes after cancellation has no handle through which it can reach the
viewport. The compatibility `submit_frame` path preserves timestamp pacing by explicitly rounding
its exact physical time up to the first eligible shared-clock coordinate.

`DroppedFramePolicy::PreserveEveryFrame` never skips. `DropLate` may cancel and skip only ordinary
playing frames, counts every skip, protects seek, scrub, step, resume, and loop-boundary frames, and
marks the positive consecutive ceiling as a forced visible presentation. Dropping is scheduling
only. It never manufactures a replacement graph value or changes cache publication.

## Audio discontinuities

The bounded output path now carries a preallocated atomic discard generation shared by its sole
producer and sole realtime consumer. Transport requests a generation and blocks producer admission
until the callback clears its consumer-owned ring and publishes the applied generation. Requests
may coalesce safely, new-epoch samples cannot be cleared with old samples, and the callback path
remains lock-free and allocation-free. Telemetry reports accepted discard requests and discarded
sample counts.

The generation acknowledges queued-sample removal at the next valid callback. It does not claim to
recall a device buffer already being presented. The current audio admission seam contains
untimestamped prepared samples, so paused, scrubbing, and ended transport admits none, while
non-normal and reverse playing transport explicitly reports muted, unsupported-rate audio instead
of playing samples at incorrect speed or direction. The existing A/V coordinator owns video hold,
correction, drop, and clock recovery against transport's live deadline, while variable-rate decoded
audio remains outside this prepared-sample seam.

## Normal, degraded, and recovery behavior

Normal playback submits the exact current frame, replans bounded nearest-first prediction, passes
the independent shared-clock deadline and live interval through A/V coordination, and immediately
schedules the next exact coordinate after presentation.
Seek, scrub, step, rate, direction, loop, pause, and resume operations cancel both foreground and
prediction generations, increment the transport epoch, request audio discard, and schedule their
protected exact target without waiting.

Viewport saturation retains the exact completed frame and exposes viewport backpressure. A frame
evaluation failure pauses clock-driven advancement, discards queued audio, and exposes frame
degradation until an explicit valid seek recovers through the same engine. Natural nonlooping end
also mutes transport and discards queued tail audio. A failed prefetch generation is reported
independently while foreground evaluation remains authoritative, and a later successful current
generation clears that degradation. Pending audio discard and unsupported audio rate remain
separately visible in every snapshot.

## Research basis

Apple AVFoundation exact seeking documents zero seek tolerance for sample-accurate selection and
its additional decode cost:
`https://developer.apple.com/documentation/avfoundation/avplayeritem/seek%28to%3Atolerancebefore%3Atoleranceafter%3Acompletionhandler%3A%29`.
GStreamer seeking defines flushing seeks as queued-data discard, accurate seeks as editor behavior,
signed rates as direction, and segment seeks as loop boundaries:
`https://gstreamer.freedesktop.org/documentation/additional/design/seeking.html`. GStreamer frame
stepping and flush-event design informed low-latency paused stepping and explicit temporary-state
clearing:
`https://gstreamer.freedesktop.org/documentation/additional/design/framestep.html` and
`https://gstreamer.freedesktop.org/documentation/plugin-development/advanced/events.html`.

## Verification

Red-first device-output contracts prove whole-queue discard, pending producer rejection,
two-generation coalescing, callback silence, exact clock progress, telemetry, and post-acknowledgement
recovery. Red-first transport contracts use the real bounded worker pool, output producer and
consumer, playback clock, prefetcher, viewport handoff, and deterministic frame evaluator. They
prove seek and scrub supersession, paused stepping, resume continuity, 0.5x and 1.5x cadence,
reverse traversal, loop wrapping, unsupported-rate audio, bounded drops, forced progress, protected
seek, foreground failure, replaceable prefetch failure, viewport backpressure, and recovery without
identity drift.

The A/V coordinator contract additionally proves that a transport-owned live deadline and
rate-adjusted interval drive scheduling while exact media PTS and nominal duration remain unchanged.
Focused playback, prefetch, graph-prefetch, clock, A/V measurement, cache prediction, timeline
retime, and audio-device contracts remain green. Package tests, documentation, warnings-denied
Clippy, formatting, dependency and boundary policy, updated codebase maps, and the repository-owned
full checkpoint verifier provide the final proof ring. Exact commands and results are retained in
the ignored checkpoint execution log.

## Remaining constraints

This checkpoint does not implement P2.W06.C007 public command and transaction dispatch. The
separate P2.W06.C005 render-export transaction consumes immutable exact-time inputs without
inheriting live transport policy. Native viewport presentation, decoded-source playback scheduling,
variable-rate audio processing, physical device latency, cached-scrub performance,
reference-playback zero-drop hardware gates, container publication, and application-shell controls
remain their existing owners or later proof lanes.
